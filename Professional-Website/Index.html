Why not college?

After graduating Justus-Tiawah, a small K-8th grade school, I attended Claremore High School. I was pretty scared jumping from a class of 55 to about 400, but I quickly made new friends. Throughout highschool I wasn't sure what I wanted to do, but that changed Junior year. I thought I wanted to go into cybersecurity but unfortunately my school didn't offer a class that taught that. Senior year, my favorite teacher told me that he was teaching a coding class and he thought I should join. I instantly found a love for coding and I was addicted to it. I would always come home and code small games such as hangman.
After doing research I found The University of Oklahoma Fullstack Academy. I had the option to go to college but I decided that I wanted to further my knowledge in coding and learn in a different style. I also have gained responsibility by having to pay monthly for school on my own. This bootcamp was one of my best decisions of my life as I met incredible teammates and people. My instructor was amazing and I am extremely lucky to have been taught by him. He gave me resources that I continue to use to help myself learn everyday. I am glad that I got real job experience that I can use in my next job!
With a certificate in web development and knowing both front-end and back-end I know that I am able to contribute to any needs that my team needs. I love front-end development and being able to be creative in any projects that the client may want. Having a knowledge in back-end development gives me an advantage and I will leverage that to the best of my ability. I am excited to be able to work for a company that gives me an opportunity and I know that I will exceed the expectations that I am given. I believe that no mountain is too high to climb, although Mount Everest may not be for me lol!
